Conclusion Page

So why is Italian animation seminal? In my opinion, it is because it allows for deeper characterization. As we have seen from each of the genres, the animated work always gave its characters more depth by truly leaning into the medium. L’Ultimo Sciuscià showed another dimension to a child’s imagination, West and Soda demonstrated just how far the boldness of a Western hero could go, and Allegro non Troppo demonstrated how live action and animation work so well together- and shown in tandem with Italian fiction cinema, demonstrates just how exaggeration can add to a character like Ugo Fantozzi.

In recent years Italian animation has achieved great things, especially in its animated TV series. And most recently it found great international success in Disney’s film “Luca” in 2020. The future of Italian animation is looking bright indeed!
